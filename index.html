LIFE EXPERIENCES

Every one of us faces adversities. It is a universal human experience. Despite this, every one of us perceives, responds, and recovers from them differently.

I, myself, undeniably find it difficult to get through hard and challenging times, especially because of my pessimistic nature. There have been times that I have failure and consequently, grief, during my stay in the university to the point that I wanted to leave. One of the instances I would never forget was me knowing that I was gonna fail the course yet I took the final exam anyway. This, in the end, made me miss the last moments of my pet dog named Cola, who I left in my parents care while I am studying in the university. If I had known of her worsening condition, I would have went home right away. This precisely was the reason why my family waited for the exam to be over before telling me. It might have hurt to be the last person to know of her death, but I understand that my family had my best interest in mind.

I still have regrets and failures that haunt me to this day. The one thing that comforts me now is believing that everything happens for a reason. Not everyone subscribes to this belief and even I have some reservations about it, but I think it would be best for me to believe. There are always more than one way to look at things. People generally either look on either the negative or positive side of things. It is fine to recognize that bad things happen, and there sometimes are really no reason for it. It's fine to think about them but it's important to know when to stop, and when to actually turn that bad thing into something good or useful to you. I still blame myself for not being there for Cola because I was the reason why I had to take the final exam. It was my ultimately my fault that I was not able to be there for her. This series of events has been a real eye-opener. I have gotten motivated to actually exert effort in my academics, just so that this kind of thing would not happen again with my other pets.

This goes to show that I may have experienced failures time and time again, and I will continue to do so, but I have yet to fail on getting back on my feet every single time.
